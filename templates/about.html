ABOUT THE ARTIST & PARAMETERS

Piet Mondrian

was a Dutch painter renowned for his role in developing the De Stijl art

movement, which emphasized simplicity and abstraction. He is best known for his abstract

works that use a grid of vertical and horizontal black lines and the three primary colors: red,

yellow, and blue. Mondrian's art evolved over his career, but he is most celebrated for these

geometric compositions, where he reduced forms and colors to their most basic elements.

His work not only had a profound influence on abstract art but also on architecture, design,

and fashion, reflecting his vision of art's role in achieving harmony and balance in life.


Parameters explanation

Horizontal Line counter: The number of horizontal lines that it will have

Vertical Line counter: The number of vertical lines that it will have

Color Distribution: The number of colors that will be displayed

Line thickness: The thickness of all the lines

Minimum Line Space: The shortest amount of space that will be created in between the lines

Horizontal Rectangle split Chance (%): The chance that a rectangle will be split horizontally.

Vertical Rectangle split chance (%): The chance that a rectangle will split vertically.

Neighbor color change (%): The chance in percentage that the color next to the rectangle will be the same as the current rectangle.

White Rectangle Chance (%): The chance in percentage that the rectangle next to the current rectangle will be white.
